Reproductive Cycle


Diagram of reproductive cycle

The Ocean Wolf reproduces sexually by producing multiple eggs. When an organism fertilizes and releases eggs it is called spawning. When the female ocean wolf releases its eggs in the ocean, the male ocean wolf then releases seeds onto the eggs which are very similar to seeds known as milt which is released from male fish. When these ocean wolf eggs hatch they are released into the ocean where they float about until the larvae become full grown ocean wolves.


Possible Genetic Problems

  • Some ocean wolves are born with birth defects.
  • Some include sea wolves growing with land fur.
    • Wolves with land fur tend to die earlier than ocean wolves without fur due to receive traits that are not fully evolved.
  • Ocean wolves can also grow with damaged gills and can die up to three days after birth.
  • Ocean wolves can even be born with short fins as a recessive trait.
    • With short fins the ocean wolf becomes slower than ocean wolves with longer fins.


Meiosis

Meiosis is a process of nuclear division. In this process four haploid daughter cells are formed. Due to the mix of random chromosomes, each daughter cell will be unique. Meiosis is also the process of making the sex cells for reproduction; sperm and eggs. Meiosis is an essential role for reproduction and the species’ survival.

Protein Synthesis

Protein synthesis is carried out through a process called translation. The DNA in the cell becomes transcribed into a mRNA (messenger RNA) molecule. In the process of transcription, the mRNA must be translated in order to create a protein. Working together, mRNA and tRNA (transfer RNA) create proteins in the cell. Protein is an essential part of many different parts of an animal's body including the bones, muscle, skin, and blood. Proteins are also used to build and repair damaged tissue and to make enzymes.
